👄 Mouth
press play
Strong = open, weak = tiny. The stressed syllable gets a big, clear vowel; the weak ones shrink to a barely-open “uh” (ə). That shrinking is what makes English sound English.

💨 Loudness = air pressure

Press play on the Conductor above. Watch the air rise from the lungs: on the stressed syllable you push a little more air, so the arrows speed up and widen and the folds swing a touch wider. Loudness comes from air, not from a higher voice.

press play
Notice how small the change is. The stressed syllable gets only ~10–20% more air — English stress rides mostly on pitch and length, not volume. The common mistake is shoving lots more air (shouting the syllable) instead of lifting the pitch.
